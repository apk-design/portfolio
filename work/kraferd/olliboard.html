Designing an On-Site Job Application & Safety Assessment Platform

This project was created for a venture capital firm's portfolio companies with high-volume frontline roles. The goal was to build an on-site job application system designed for walk-in candidates. The platform streamlined the application process and embedded a mandatory safety-oriented quiz to help identify candidates with strong situational awareness and responsibility.

UI/UX Web Design On Site Job Applications

Problem

Many applicants applied in person without resumes or prior preparation. Paper applications were inconsistent, slow to process, and failed to test whether a candidate demonstrated the safety awareness required for warehouse, manufacturing, or transportation roles.

The goal was to create a digital flow that:

  • Guided candidates through a clean, simple application
  • Ensured all required information was collected
  • Evaluated basic safety judgment using scenario-based questions
  • Provided an instant ‚Äúpass/fail‚Äù status to hiring staff

System Overview

The tool was designed as a single unified, multi-step flow:

  1. Welcome & orientation
  2. Basic personal information collection
  3. Job role selection and availability
  4. Experience questionnaires
  5. Safety quiz with scenario-based questions
  6. Review & submission
  7. Pass/fail feedback screen

User Personas

Before defining the application flow, I created a set of representative personas based on real candidate profiles observed at the VC firm's portfolio companies. These personas helped clarify reading levels, technology comfort, preferred channels, and the specific pain points candidates experienced during on-site job applications.

Welcome & Start Screen

The entry point introduced the company and set the tone for an encouraging, low-friction experience. Candidates could begin the application immediately on a tablet or kiosk device.

Step 1: Personal Information

A minimal set of fields kept the barrier to entry low while ensuring employers had essential contact details. The UI focused on large touch targets and clear instructions.

Step 2: Availability

Candidates selected the days and times they were available to work. The layout allowed quick scanning of weekly patterns and simplified part-time or rotating shift arrangements.

Step 3: Work Experience & Background

The experience section captured relevant background information, previous job roles, and employment history. Questions were designed to be short and structured to minimize time spent typing on a touch device.

Step 4: Safety Quiz

This was the core differentiator of the system. Candidates completed a scenario-based safety quiz that tested practical judgment and awareness.

The visual design treated the quiz like an assessment, with clear multiple-choice options and a simple step-by-step structure to reduce cognitive load.

Optional: Document or Photo Upload

Candidates could optionally upload a photo of certifications or documentation. This was added for companies requiring forklift certification, ID verification, or training proof.

Step 5: Review & Submit

The review page displayed all entered information in a structured, printable layout. Candidates could make corrections before final submission.

Impact

The final product created a standardized, repeatable on-site application system that:

  • Reduced paperwork and manual data entry
  • Filtered candidates based on safety awareness
  • Shortened time-to-hire for frontline roles
  • Improved candidate clarity and confidence
  • Gave hiring managers immediate, structured insights
