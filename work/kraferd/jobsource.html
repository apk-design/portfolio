JobSource ‚Äì Multi-sided hiring platform

JobSource was a concept-stage hiring platform I designed while working at a VC firm. The goal was to create something investors could immediately understand as a scalable product: a system where job seekers could maintain rich, structured profiles; employers could quickly evaluate candidates; and the platform could extend into new channels like mobile and digital wallets for real-world check-ins and events.

My role covered product strategy, UX flows, and high-fidelity UI for candidate, employer, and admin experiences, as well as explorations in Apple Wallet and Apple Watch.

Candidate Experience ‚Äì Desktop Profile

For candidates, JobSource centered on a profile that felt more like a living portfolio than a static r√©sum√©. The layout gives a quick snapshot‚Äîname, title, location, and a compact overview card‚Äîfollowed by stacked sections for work experience, education, skills, and achievements. Each block is modular and scannable so recruiters can scroll and get a complete picture without losing context.

I used consistent card structures and subtle dividers to keep long-form content readable, while the right column surfaces tags and filters that connect directly to matching jobs and search facets.

Candidate Experience ‚Äì Guided Profile Editing

Because this was also an investor pitch, I designed the editing flows to tell a story about data quality. Candidates can add or edit sections in focused modals, with clear labels for role, dates, company, responsibilities, and outcomes. Rather than a single intimidating form, the profile grows through repeatable patterns: one card per experience, one modal per action.

This structure makes it easy to imagine onboarding flows, automated prompts to fill gaps, and future scoring of profile completeness‚Äîall important to highlight for investors.

Mobile Experience ‚Äì Job Search On the Go

The mobile prototype translated the same profile concepts into a format that works on smaller screens and in short sessions. Candidates can swipe through their profile, review experiences, and quickly update fields without digging through nested menus.

The layout preserves the core hierarchy‚Äîsummary, experience, skills‚Äîwhile using bottom actions and large tap targets to support one-handed use. This helped demonstrate how JobSource could be a companion app for ongoing career management rather than a one-time sign-up.

Employer & Admin Tools ‚Äì Structured Job Data

On the employer side, I designed admin tools that make it easy to keep job and department data clean. Forms are simplified into a single-column layout with clear groupings for department, role, location, and status, plus validation around required fields.

This view demonstrates how JobSource could scale with larger organizations: structured fields enable consistent job postings, better search, and analytics on hiring across departments‚Äîkey talking points for investors evaluating the business model.

Cross-Channel Extension ‚Äì Apple Watch & Wallet

To show how JobSource could extend beyond the browser, I explored how candidates might use a JobSource pass inside Apple Wallet on Apple Watch. The concept turns a candidate profile into a scannable card with their photo, title, and location, plus a QR code that can be used for check-ins at job fairs, interviews, or on-site security gates.

This positions JobSource not only as a job board, but as infrastructure that can support real-world hiring workflows.

Cross-Channel Extension ‚Äì Wallet on iPhone

On iPhone, the JobSource pass behaves like a digital ID: candidates can surface their card from Wallet, present a QR code, and view basic activity‚Äîsuch as recent check-ins or events. Additional flows explore how candidates might manage notification settings or view hiring activity tied to their pass.

These explorations helped frame JobSource as a platform capable of integrating with enterprise systems, attendance tracking, and event analytics‚Äîimportant levers in an investor conversation about long-term value.

Outcomes & Reflection

Although JobSource remained a concept-stage product, the work gave the VC firm a concrete artifact to use in investor conversations. The prototype demonstrates a cohesive system: structured candidate data, maintainable employer tools, and cross-channel extensions into mobile and wallet experiences.

For my own practice, this project reinforced how important it is to design for multiple stakeholders in parallel‚Äîcandidates, employers, and platform owners‚Äîand to show investors how those pieces connect in a scalable way.
