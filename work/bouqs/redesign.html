Context & Role

While working with The Bouqs, I was asked to explore a full-site refresh. The existing experience felt busy and dated, with limited hierarchy and inconsistent use of imagery. My role covered UX, visual design, and interaction design from early exploration through high-fidelity comps.

Problem & Goals

Customers loved the product photography but often struggled to scan collections, compare options, and understand what they were buying for important occasions (weddings, events, gifts). The site also needed better accessibility and a more modern brand expression.

  • Clarify page hierarchy so shoppers can quickly find the right bouquet or collection.
  • Make product cards and detail pages easier to scan and compare.
  • Elevate the brand with a calmer, wedding-friendly visual language.
  • Improve accessibility via contrast, type scale, and more structured layouts.

Exploration ‚Äì Concept V1

The first concept focused on refreshing the existing structure:

  • Introduced a soft sage and cream palette to better match the brand‚Äôs organic, floral feel.
  • Cleaned up the navigation bar and hero area to highlight key collections.
  • Updated product cards with more breathing room and tighter typography.
  • Standardized photo ratios and card layouts for a more consistent grid.

V1 already made the site calmer and more cohesive, but it still felt close to the original layout and didn‚Äôt fully leverage storytelling or clear shopping paths for different use cases (weddings, occasions, build-your-own bundles).

Final Direction ‚Äì Concept V2

The second concept pushed further, reorganizing the experience around collections and occasions and leaning into storytelling with large editorial photography.

  • Homepage as story, not catalog. Larger hero photography with clear headline and CTA, followed by curated collections (‚ÄúWedding Bundles‚Äù, ‚ÄúBuild Your Own Floral Package‚Äù) instead of a long undifferentiated product grid.
  • Collection-first layout. Dedicated collection pages group bouquets by style and palette, each with simple filters on the left and a clean product grid on the right.
  • Refined product detail pages. Bigger hero image, simplified information hierarchy (name, price, size, delivery details), and clear ‚ÄúAdd to Cart‚Äù actions above the fold.
  • Editorial content modules. ‚ÄúHow it works‚Äù and inspiration blocks use lifestyle photography to help shoppers imagine how arrangements will look in real events.

Key UX & Visual Changes from V1 to V2

Between the first and second concept, I systematically refined the experience to be more accessible, modern, and task-focused:

  • Stronger visual hierarchy. Headings, subheadings, and body text now have clearer size and weight differences. Cards are grouped into distinct rows/sections, which makes scan paths more predictable.
  • More deliberate use of color. The V2 palette leans on a deeper green for CTAs and section headers, improving contrast and drawing attention to primary actions, while still feeling soft and botanical.
  • Improved card design. Product tiles show consistent photo sizes, concise names, pricing, and key badges (e.g., best seller). Spacing was increased to reduce visual noise and help on smaller screens.
  • Accessibility upgrades. Raised color contrast in buttons and text, increased base font sizes, and simplified background overlays behind text on photography to meet readability guidelines.
  • Clearer primary CTAs. Prominent ‚ÄúShop the Collection‚Äù and ‚ÄúBuild Your Own Bouquet‚Äù buttons appear in hero modules and collection intros, giving shoppers obvious next steps.
  • Content structure for different journeys. The redesign separates inspiration (editorial imagery, real weddings) from shopping tasks (filters, grid, PDP), so browsing feels enjoyable but decision-making remains simple.
